**The Pod** is a digital-innovation hub in **Brabant Wallon** — a place for coworking, events, and hands-on tech training, from **blockchain** to **cybersecurity**. We built its website.

<video src="/assets/content/the-pod-bw.mp4" controls playsinline preload="metadata" poster="/assets/content/the-pod-bw-still.webp"></video>

## The challenge

A hub like The Pod does several things at once — coworking, a rolling events calendar, and a constantly-changing catalogue of training courses — and its site has to make all of that legible to a broad regional audience while staying easy for the team to keep current as the programme evolves.

## What we built

Delivered at Cherry Pulp on **WordPress** (**Bedrock**):

- A public site presenting The Pod's coworking, events, and training offer
- A structured **course catalogue** covering the hub's tech programme — blockchain, cybersecurity, and more
- Editorial structures the team can maintain themselves as the offer changes

## My role

I worked on the **WordPress** build — backend and frontend — at Cherry Pulp, on the structured, regularly-updated content behind the hub's public site.

## The interesting part

The real tension in a training hub's site is content velocity: it publishes constantly — new courses, new events, new sessions — so the site's value depends less on any single page than on how easily non-technical staff can keep it current. Getting the **WordPress** content model right for that continuous editing, on a **Bedrock**-based stack, was the core of the work.

## Outcome

The Pod got a site that presents its coworking, events, and training in one place, maintainable by its own team — the front door to a regional digital hub. The site has since been rebuilt by another team.
